# Parameters 
$p_{commodity\_lodf\_tolerance}$ 
& The minimum absolute value of the line outage distribution factor (LODF) that is considered meaningful. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $c \in commodity$ \\ 

$p_{commodity\_physics}$ 
& Defines if the commodity follows lodf or ptdf physics. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $c \in commodity$ \\ 

$p_{commodity\_ptdf\_threshold}$ 
& The minimum absolute value of the power transfer distribution factor (PTDF) that is considered meaningful. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $c \in commodity$ \\ 

$p_{candidate\_connections}$ 
& The number of connections that may be invested in \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_availability\_factor}$ 
& Availability of the connection, acting as a multiplier on its connection\_capacity. Typically between 0-1. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_contingency}$ 
& A boolean flag for defining a contingency connection. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_flow\_cost}$ 
& Variable costs of a flow through a connection. E.g. EUR/MWh of energy throughput. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_investment\_cost}$ 
& The per unit investment cost for the connection over the connection\_investment\_lifetime \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_investment\_lifetime}$ 
& Determines the minimum investment lifetime of a connection. Once invested, it remains in service for this long \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_investment\_variable\_type}$ 
& Determines whether the investment variable is integer variable\_type\_integer or continuous variable\_type\_continuous \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_monitored}$ 
& A boolean flag for defining a contingency connection. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_reactance}$ 
& The per unit reactance of a connection. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_reactance\_base}$ 
& If the reactance is given for a p.u.  (e.g. p.u. = 100MW), the connection\_reactance\_base can be set to perform this conversion (e.g. *100). \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_resistance}$ 
& The per unit resistance of a connection. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{connection\_type}$ 
& A selector between a normal and a lossless bidirectional connection. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{fix\_connections\_invested}$ 
& Setting a value fixes the connections\_invested variable accordingly \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{fix\_connections\_invested\_available}$ 
& Setting a value fixes the connections\_invested\_available variable accordingly \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{has\_binary\_gas\_flow}$ 
& This parameter needs to be set to true in order to represent bidirectional pressure drive gas transfer. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $conn \in connection$ \\ 

$p_{duration\_unit}$ 
& Defines the base temporal unit of the model. Currently supported values are either an hour or a minute. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{max\_gap}$ 
& Specifies the maximum optimality gap for the model. Currently only used for the master problem within a decomposed structure \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{max\_iterations}$ 
& Specifies the maximum number of iterations for the model. Currently only used for the master problem within a decomposed structure \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{model\_end}$ 
& Defines the last timestamp to be modelled. Rolling optimisation terminates after passing this point. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{model\_start}$ 
& Defines the first timestamp to be modelled. Relative temporal\_blocks refer to this value for their start and end. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{model\_type}$ 
& Used to identify model objects as relating to the master problem or operational sub problems (default) \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{roll\_forward}$ 
& Defines how much the model moves ahead in time between solves in a rolling optimisation. Without this parameter, everything is solved in as a single optimisation. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{write\_lodf\_file}$ 
& A boolean flag for whether the LODF values should be written to a results file. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{write\_mps\_file}$ 
& A selector for writing an .mps file of the model. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{write\_ptdf\_file}$ 
& A boolean flag for whether the LODF values should be written to a results file. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{big\_m}$ 
& Sufficiently large number used for linearization bilinear terms, e.g. to enforce bidirectional flow for gas pipielines \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $m \in model$ \\ 

$p_{balance\_type}$ 
& A selector for how the :nodal\_balance constraint should be handled. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{candidate\_storages}$ 
& Determines the maximum number of new storages which may be invested in. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{demand}$ 
& Demand for the commodity of a node. Energy gains can be represented using negative demand. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{downward\_reserve}$ 
& Identifier for nodes providing downward reserves \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{fix\_node\_state}$ 
& Fixes the corresponding node\_state variable to the provided value. Can be used for e.g. fixing boundary conditions. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{fix\_storages\_invested}$ 
& Used to fix the value of the storages\_invested variable \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{fix\_storages\_invested\_available}$ 
& Used to fix the value of the storages\_invested\_available variable \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{fix\_node\_pressure}$ 
& Fixes the corresponding node\_pressure variable to the provided value \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{fix\_node\_voltage\_angle}$ 
& Fixes the corresponding node\_voltage\_angle variable to the provided value \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{frac\_state\_loss}$ 
& Self-discharge coefficient for node\_state variables. Effectively, represents the *loss power per unit of state*. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{fractional\_demand}$ 
& The fraction of a node group's demand applied for the node in question. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{has\_state}$ 
& A boolean flag for whether a node has a node\_state variable. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{has\_pressure}$ 
& A boolean flag for whether a node has a node\_pressure variable. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{has\_voltage\_angle}$ 
& A boolean flag for whether a node has a node\_voltage\_angle variable. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{is\_reserve\_node}$ 
& A boolean flag for whether a node is acting as a reserve\_node \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{minimum\_reserve\_activation\_time}$ 
& Duration a certain reserve product needs to be online/available \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{nodal\_balance\_sense}$ 
& A selector for nodal\_balance constraint sense. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{node\_opf\_type}$ 
& A selector for the reference node (slack bus) when PTDF-based DC load-flow is enabled. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{node\_slack\_penalty}$ 
& A penalty cost for node\_slack\_pos and node\_slack\_neg variables. The slack variables won't be included in the model unless there's a cost defined for them. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{node\_state\_cap}$ 
& The maximum permitted value for a node\_state variable. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{node\_state\_min}$ 
& The minimum permitted value for a node\_state variable. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{state\_coeff}$ 
& Represents the commodity content of a node\_state variable in respect to the unit\_flow and connection\_flow variables. Essentially, acts as a coefficient on the node\_state variable in the :node\_injection constraint. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{storage\_investment\_cost}$ 
& Determines the investment cost per unit state\_cap over the investment life of a storage \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{storage\_investment\_lifetime}$ 
& Minimum lifetime for storage investment decisions. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{storage\_investment\_variable\_type}$ 
& Determines whether the storage investment variable is continuous (usually representing capacity) or integer (representing discrete units invested) \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{tax\_in\_unit\_flow}$ 
& Tax costs for incoming unit\_flows on this node. E.g. EUR/MWh. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{tax\_net\_unit\_flow}$ 
& Tax costs for net incoming and outgoing unit\_flows on this node. Incoming flows accrue positive net taxes, and outgoing flows accrue negative net taxes. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{tax\_out\_unit\_flow}$ 
& Tax costs for outgoing unit\_flows from this node. E.g. EUR/MWh. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{upward\_reserve}$ 
& Identifier for nodes providing upward reserves \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{max\_node\_pressure}$ 
& Maximum allowed gas pressure at node. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{min\_node\_pressure}$ 
& Minimum allowed gas pressure at node. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{max\_voltage\_angle}$ 
& Maximum allowed voltage angle at node. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{min\_voltage\_angle}$ 
& Minimum allowed voltage angle at node.  \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $n \in node$ \\ 

$p_{block\_end}$ 
& The end time for the temporal\_block. Can be given either as a DateTime for a static end point, or as a Duration for an end point relative to the start of the current optimisation. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $tb \in temporal\_block$ \\ 

$p_{block\_start}$ 
& The start time for the temporal\_block. Can be given either as a DateTime for a static start point, or as a Duration for an start point relative to the start of the current optimisation. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $tb \in temporal\_block$ \\ 

$p_{resolution}$ 
& Temporal resolution of the temporal\_block. Essentially, divides the period between block\_start and block\_end into TimeSlices with the input resolution. \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $tb \in temporal\_block$ \\ 

$p_{weight}$ 
& Weighting factor of the temporal block associated with the objective function \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $tb \in temporal\_block$ \\ 

$p_{representative\_periods\_mapping}$ 
& Mapping from real timelices to their corresponding representative days \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $tb \in temporal\_block$ \\ 

$p_{candidate\_units}$ 
& Number of units which may be additionally constructed \\ 
\text{-Inherent Index:} $u \in unit$ \\ 

$p_{curtailment\_cost}$

\text{-Inherent Index:} $u \in unit$ \\
